Kalighat Kali Temple

History:
The Kalighat Kali Temple in Kolkata is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, dedicated to Goddess Kali. The current temple structure was completed in the early 19th century in the traditional ath-chala Bengal architectural style. The temple has deep spiritual significance and features intricate terracotta motifs and historical elements preserved through recent renovations.
Puja Rituals:
Devotees partake in daily pujas which include offerings of flowers, fruits, and chanting of hymns. The temple also hosts special rituals during important Hindu festivals honoring Goddess Kali.
Festivals Celebrated:
Kali Puja and Diwali are the most significant festivals, celebrated with grandeur and attracting massive crowds. Other local festivals and cultural events keep the temple spiritually vibrant throughout the year.
