Ugadi Festival

Reason for Ugadi

Ugadi marks the beginning of the Hindu New Year as per the lunisolar calendar, primarily celebrated in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka. The festival signifies new beginnings and the arrival of spring, a time for hope, renewal, and cultural heritage. The word “Ugadi” comes from Sanskrit meaning “the beginning of a new age.”

Celebrations During Ugadi

Ugadi celebrations include a range of customs and rituals:

  • Pachadi Preparation: A special dish made with jaggery, tamarind, neem, and other tastes symbolizing different emotions in life.
  • House Cleaning and Decoration: Homes are cleaned and decorated with mango leaves and rangoli.
  • Prayer and Puja: Devotees perform prayers to seek blessings for prosperity and health.
  • New Clothes and Gifts: Wearing new traditional attire and exchanging gifts.
  • Cultural Programs: Music, dance, and recitations of classical and spiritual texts.

Ugadi is celebrated as a joyous and auspicious festival heralding fresh starts and harmony.
