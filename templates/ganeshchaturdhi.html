Ganesh Chaturthi

Reason for Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a Hindu festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity known as the remover of obstacles and the god of beginnings and wisdom. The festival honors Ganesha’s blessings for prosperity, health, and success.

Celebrations During Ganesh Chaturthi

The festival is marked by various joyful traditions, including:

  • Installation of Ganesha idols in homes and public places, beautifully decorated and adorned.
  • Pujas and Aartis: Devotees perform prayers, chanting, and singing devotional songs daily.
  • Cultural events and community programs like dance, drama, and music performances.
  • Modak preparation: Sweet dumplings considered Lord Ganesha’s favorite treat.
  • Visarjan: On the final day, idols are immersed in water bodies in grand processions, symbolizing Ganesha’s journey back to Mount Kailash.

Ganesh Chaturthi strengthens communal harmony, spirituality, and cultural traditions.
