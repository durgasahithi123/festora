Mahavir Jayanti

Significance of Mahavir Jayanti

Mahavir Jayanti celebrates the birth of Lord Mahavira, the 24th and last Tirthankara in Jainism, who revitalized and propagated the Jain philosophy of non-violence, truth, and asceticism. It is one of the most important festivals in Jainism, symbolizing spiritual awakening and ethical living.

Celebrations During Mahavir Jayanti

The festival is observed with various spiritual and cultural activities such as:

  • Processions: Colorful parades featuring images or statues of Lord Mahavira accompanied by chanting and music.
  • Temple Visits: Devotees gather in Jain temples for prayers, meditation, and sermons on Mahaviraâ€™s teachings.
  • Offerings: Flowers, fruits, and sweets are offered in temples.
  • Community Service: Acts of charity, feeding the poor, and environmental initiatives.
  • Fasting and Reflection: Many Jains observe fasting and reflect on the path of righteousness.

Mahavir Jayanti inspires followers to follow the principles of compassion, honesty, and self-discipline taught by Lord Mahavira.
