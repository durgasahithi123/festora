Shree Somnath Temple

History:
Shree Somnath Temple in Gujarat is one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is an ancient temple with a history of several millennia, having been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. The current grand structure was constructed in the 1950s in the Māru-Gurjara architectural style, showcasing intricate carvings and splendid craftsmanship.
Puja Rituals:
Daily worship at Somnath includes multiple aartis like Mangala, Bhog, and Sandhya, with priests performing elaborate rituals. Devotees offer flowers, milk, water, and perform chanting to please Lord Shiva, enveloped by the temple’s deep spiritual atmosphere.
Festivals Celebrated:
Maha Shivaratri is the highlight festival celebrated with great devotion, including night-long prayers and cultural programs. Other notable festivals are Kartik Purnima and Shravan Maas, attracting thousands of pilgrims from across India to the temple.
