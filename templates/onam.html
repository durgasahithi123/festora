Onam Festival

Reason for Onam

Onam is a major annual harvest festival celebrated in Kerala, India. It marks the homecoming of the mythical King Mahabali, whose reign is remembered as a golden era of prosperity and happiness. The festival symbolizes the victory of good over evil and the spirit of unity and cultural pride among Malayalis.

Celebrations During Onam

Onam festivities are vibrant and extensive, including:

  • Pookalam: Intricate floral rangoli designs made at the entrance of homes.
  • Onasadya: A grand vegetarian feast served on banana leaves with a variety of traditional dishes.
  • Vallam Kali: The famous snake boat races held in Kerala’s backwaters.
  • Traditional Dances: Performances like Thiruvathira, Kaikottikali, and Pulikali (tiger dance).
  • Cultural Programs: Showcasing folk music, arts, and temple prayers.

These celebrations emphasize community bonding, thanksgiving for a good harvest, and continuation of Kerala’s rich heritage.
