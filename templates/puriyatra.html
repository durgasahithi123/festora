Puri Rath Yatra

About Puri Rath Yatra

Puri Rath Yatra, also known as the Chariot Festival, is an iconic Hindu festival held annually in Puri, Odisha. It celebrates the journey of Lord Jagannath along with his siblings Balabhadra and Subhadra from the Jagannath Temple to the Gundicha Temple. The deities travel on gigantic, elaborately decorated chariots pulled by thousands of devotees.

This festival signifies the accessibility of God to all devotees and the unity of humanity through shared spiritual fervor. Millions gather to witness and partake in this grand event, revered as one of the ‘Char Dham’ pilgrimages.

Festival Celebrations

The festival involves elaborate preparations followed by several days of festivities:

  • Construction of three distinct, massive chariots for Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra.
  • The procession of chariots down the main streets, attracting devotees who pull the chariots as a sacred act.
  • Daily rituals, chanting of sacred hymns, and offerings at the Gundicha Temple during their stay.
  • Religious dances, music, and cultural programs that showcase Odisha's rich heritage.
  • The return journey (Bahuda Yatra) where the deities are brought back to the Jagannath Temple after days of celebrations.

The Rath Yatra embodies devotion, community, and cultural pride sustained through centuries.
