Sabarimala Temple

History:
Sabarimala Temple, located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a famous shrine dedicated to Lord Ayyappa. It is one of the largest annual pilgrimage sites globally, attracting millions of devotees during the Mandala season.
Puja Rituals:
Devotees undertake rigorous austerities before making the pilgrimage. The temple rituals are performed according to ancient traditions, emphasizing spirituality and devotion to Lord Ayyappa.
Festivals Celebrated:
The Mandala-Makaravilakku season spanning November to January is the most important festival, with special ceremonies and large crowds. Other seasonal festivals reinforce devotion and community spirit among pilgrims.
