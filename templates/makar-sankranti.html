Makara Sankranti

Reason for Makara Sankranti

Makara Sankranti is a Hindu harvest festival celebrated to mark the transition of the Sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara). It symbolizes the end of winter and the beginning of longer days, heralding the arrival of the harvest season. The festival is associated with prosperity, new beginnings, and spiritual cleansing.

Celebrations During Makara Sankranti

The festival is observed with various vibrant customs including:

  • Kite Flying: Flying colorful kites symbolizing joy and freedom is a popular activity.
  • Holy Dips: Devotees take ritualistic dips in rivers like the Ganges to purify themselves.
  • Sweets and Feasts: Special dishes made from sesame seeds (til) and jaggery are prepared and shared.
  • Social Gatherings: Families and communities come together for prayers, feasting, and cultural events.

Makara Sankranti promotes gratitude for seasonal change and fosters unity and devotion.
