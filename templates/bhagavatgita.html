Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the Indian epic Mahabharata. It is a sacred text of the Hindu religion, presenting a conversation between prince Arjuna and the god Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. The Gita addresses the moral, ethical, and spiritual dilemmas faced by Arjuna on the battlefield, exploring paths to self-realization and duty.

The Bhagavad Gita is considered one of the most important philosophical classics and continues to inspire people globally on matters of life, duty, and spirituality.
