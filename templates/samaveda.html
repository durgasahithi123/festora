Samarveda

The Samaveda is the Veda of melodies and chants, one of the four sacred Vedas of Hinduism. It contains about 1,875 verses, most of which are derived from the Rigveda but arranged musically for singing during rituals and ceremonies.
The Samaveda is composed of two main parts:
Gana (melody collections) used for public and meditative chanting.
Arcika (verse books) which correspond to verses set to those melodies.
