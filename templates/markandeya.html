Markandeya Purana

The Markandeya Purana is one of the eighteen major Puranas in Hinduism, consisting of 137 chapters and around 6,900 to 9,000 verses. It is attributed to the sage Markandeya and contains a wide range of topics including mythology, cosmology, philosophy, dharma (duty), karma, sacred geography, and rituals.
The Purana begins with sage Jaimini asking Markandeya questions that were raised but not answered in the Mahabharata. The text includes moral teachings intertwined with mythological narratives and presents Yoga philosophy as a path to liberation. The latter portion records genealogies, descriptions of cosmic cycles (manvantaras), and glorifies deities like Surya (Sun god). It also recounts stories including that of King Harischandra.
