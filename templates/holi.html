Holi Festival

Reason for Holi

Holi, known as the Festival of Colors, celebrates the triumph of good over evil and the arrival of spring. Rooted in Hindu mythology, it commemorates the story of Prahladaâ€™s unwavering devotion to Lord Vishnu and the burning of the demoness Holika. The festival signifies love, forgiveness, and the renewal of relationships.

Celebrations During Holi

Holi is celebrated with vibrant and joyous activities such as:

  • Throwing colored powders (gulal) and colored water on friends and strangers.
  • Bonfires on the eve of Holi symbolizing the destruction of evil (Holika Dahan).
  • Singing and dancing in the streets accompanied by traditional music and drums.
  • Feasting with festive sweets like gujiya and thandai drinks.
  • Community bonding promoting joy, unity, and cultural heritage.

Holi spreads happiness and brings people together beyond social and cultural boundaries.
