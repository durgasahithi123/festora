Tirupati Balaji Temple

History:
The Tirumala Venkateswara Temple in Andhra Pradesh is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Vishnu. It is one of the richest and most visited temples in the world, with a history spanning several centuries. The temple follows the Vaikhanasa Agama tradition, and its origins trace back to ancient Hindu texts and legends.
Puja Rituals:
Daily rituals include multiple aartis, Suprabhata Seva, Thomala Seva, Archana and the famous Laddu Prasadam offering. Devotees participate in hair tonsuring as an act of humility and surrender. Rituals uphold ancient traditions throughout the year.
Festivals Celebrated:
Brahmotsavam, a nine-day annual festival, is the grandest event with processions on various vahanams. Other major festivals are Vaikunta Ekadasi, Rathasapthami, Teppotsavam (float festival), and Rama Navami.
