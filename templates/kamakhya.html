Kamakhya Shakti Peetha

History:
Kamakhya Temple situated on the Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, Assam, is one of the oldest and most revered Shakti Peethas in India. The current temple was rebuilt in 1565 under the Koch dynasty, blending Nagara and indigenous Assamese architectural styles featuring a distinctive beehive-shaped dome. It houses the sacred yoni-shaped stone in the sanctum, symbolizing the goddess Kamakhya and is central to tantric worship traditions.

Festivals Celebrated:
The Ambubachi Mela, celebrated annually in June, marks the menstruation period of the goddess and draws thousands of pilgrims. Navratri is another major festival with continuous worship and cultural events. Many devotees also visit during the Durga Puja and Dussehra festivals celebrated nearby.
