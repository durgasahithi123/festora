Magha Puja Festival

Significance of Magha Puja

Magha Puja, also known as Sangha Day, is an important Buddhist festival celebrated on the full moon day of the third lunar month (usually February or March). It commemorates the spontaneous gathering of 1,250 enlightened disciples who assembled to hear the Buddha's teachings without prior summons. The festival highlights the unity of the Buddhist community and the importance of following the Buddhaâ€™s teachings.

Celebrations During Magha Puja

Magha Puja is observed with devotion and communal activities, including:

  • Temple visits: Devotees gather to listen to sermons and chant Buddhist scriptures.
  • Offerings: Flowers, candles, and incense are offered at statues of the Buddha.
  • Merit-making: Acts of charity, meditation, and observing precepts.
  • Processions and Candlelight Vigils: Communities often hold evening marches carrying candles and lanterns symbolizing enlightenment.

The festival encourages reflection on the teachings and fostering harmony within the Sangha (Buddhist community).
