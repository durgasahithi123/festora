Jagannath Temple, Puri

History:
The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is a historic temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Vishnu. It was constructed primarily in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva. The temple is famous for its unique wooden idols and the grand annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival).
Puja Rituals:
The temple rituals include daily aartis, offering of Mahaprasad, and periodic idol renewal rituals called Nabakalebara. Devotees participate in vibrant bhajans and community prayers, keeping alive the templeâ€™s living traditions.
Festivals Celebrated:
The Rath Yatra is the most famous festival, held annually where deities are taken in chariots for public procession. Other important festivals include Snana Yatra, Chandan Yatra, and the various deva dances.
