Eid Festival (Eid al-Fitr)

Reason for Eid

Eid al-Fitr is a major Islamic festival celebrated by Muslims worldwide marking the end of Ramadan, the month of fasting. It is a day of gratitude to Allah for the strength to complete the fast and for forgiveness and blessings received. The festival promotes charity, community, and spiritual renewal.

Celebrations During Eid

Typical customs and practices during Eid al-Fitr include:

  • Eid Prayer: Muslims gather in mosques or open grounds for a special communal prayer.
  • Charity (Zakat al-Fitr): Giving to the needy to purify wealth and to enable all to celebrate.
  • Feasting: Families gather to enjoy festive meals and sweets.
  • New Clothes and Gifts: Wearing new attire and exchanging gifts to express joy.
  • Social Visits: Visiting relatives and neighbors, strengthening community bonds.

Eid is a time of joy, compassion, and reaffirmation of faith.
