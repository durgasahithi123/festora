Brahmavaivarta Purana

The Brahmavaivarta Purana is one of the major eighteen Mahapuranas in Hinduism, recognized particularly as a significant Vaishnava text. It is a voluminous Sanskrit scripture centered predominantly on the divine love and play of Radha and Krishna, presenting Krishna as the supreme reality (Brahman) and Radha as the primal energy or Shakti.
The Purana is divided into four Khandas (sections):
Brahma Khanda: Discusses Brahman (supreme spirit).
Prakriti Khanda: Focuses on Prakriti (nature/feminine divine), describing various goddesses like Radha, Durga, Lakshmi, Saraswati.
Ganesha Khanda: Narrates the life and stories of Ganesha, considered an incarnation of Krishna here.
Krishna Khanda: Richly details Radha-Krishna legends, hymns, and devotional themes.
