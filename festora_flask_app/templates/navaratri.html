Navaratri Festival

Reason for Navaratri

Navaratri is a nine-night Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga and her various forms. It celebrates the victory of good over evil, signifying Goddess Durga’s triumph over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. It is a time for spiritual renewal, devotion, and cultural expression across India and other parts of the world.

Celebrations During Navaratri

Navaratri is celebrated with vibrant rituals and cultural events, including:

  • Durga Puja: Worship of Goddess Durga with prayers, offerings, and artistic displays of her idol.
  • Garba and Dandiya Raas: Traditional folk dances performed in groups, especially popular in Gujarat and Maharashtra.
  • Fasting: Many devotees fast during the nine days as a mark of spiritual discipline.
  • Kanya Puja: Worship of young girls symbolizing the Goddess’s nine forms on the eighth or ninth day.
  • Feasting: Community meals and special festive cuisine mark the occasion.

Navaratri culminates in Vijayadashami (Dussehra), celebrating the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana and the triumph of virtue.
