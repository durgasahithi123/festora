Vaisakhi Festival

Significance of Vaisakhi

Vaisakhi, celebrated on April 14th every year, marks the founding of the Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh Ji in 1699 and is also observed as the Punjabi New Year. It is a harvest festival celebrating prosperity, renewal, and community spirit. This festival holds great religious and cultural significance for Sikhs.

Celebrations During Vaisakhi

The festival is marked by joyous events and traditional rituals such as:

  • Nagar Kirtan: Colorful processions with singing of hymns, martial arts demonstrations, and community participation.
  • Special Prayers: Devotees gather in gurdwaras for Akhand Path and Ardaas prayers.
  • Langar: Community meals served to all irrespective of caste, creed, or religion.
