Kurma Purana

The Kurma Purana is one of the eighteen Mahapuranas in Hinduism, named after the Kurma avatar, the tortoise incarnation of Lord Vishnu. It is a medieval-era text that combines Vaishnavism themes with extensive reverence for Shiva and Shakti, reflecting a balanced devotional outlook.
The Purana includes legends, mythology, theology, pilgrimage (Tirtha) guides, cosmology, and a philosophical section called the Ishvara Gita, where Shiva imparts wisdom similar to the Bhagavad Gita. It discusses concepts like Atman (self), Brahman (ultimate reality), Maya (illusion), and Moksha (liberation) based on Advaita Vedanta philosophy.
