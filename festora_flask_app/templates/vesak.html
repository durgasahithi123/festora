Vesak Festival (Buddha Purnima)

Significance of Vesak

Vesak, also known as Buddha Purnima, is the most sacred Buddhist festival celebrating the birth, enlightenment (nirvana), and passing away (parinirvana) of Gautama Buddha. It falls on the full moon day of the Vaisakha month (April or May). The festival is a time for reflection on Buddha's teachings and practice of compassion, peace, and mindfulness.

Celebrations During Vesak

Vesak is observed with various devotional and community activities, including:

  • Temple Visits: Devotees visit Buddhist temples to pray and meditate.
  • Offerings: Flowers, incense, and candles are offered to Buddha statues.
  • Chanting and Sermons: Recitations of Buddhist scriptures and sermons on Buddha's life and teachings.
  • Acts of Kindness: Charity, feeding the needy, and doing good deeds.
  • Lighting Lanterns and Decorations: Streets and temples are decorated with colorful lanterns symbolizing enlightenment.

Vesak inspires Buddhists to uphold the values of peace, wisdom, and compassion in daily life.
