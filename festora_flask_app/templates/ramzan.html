Ramzan (Ramadan)

Reason for Ramzan

Ramzan, or Ramadan, is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a holy month of fasting, prayer, and reflection. It commemorates the first revelation of the Quran to Prophet Muhammad. Muslims fast from dawn to sunset to cultivate spiritual discipline and empathy for the less fortunate.

Observances During Ramzan

Ramzan includes various spiritual and community practices such as:

  • Fasting (Sawm): Abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs during daylight hours.
  • Taraweeh Prayers: Special nightly prayers reciting the Quran.
  • Charity (Zakat and Sadaqah): Giving to the needy as a purification of wealth and expression of compassion.
  • Increased Quran Recitation and Reflection: Spiritual study and contemplation.
  • Breaking Fast (Iftar): Evening gatherings breaking the fast with dates and meals.

The month culminates with the joyous celebration of Eid al-Fitr, marking the end of fasting and thanksgiving.
