Diwali Festival

Reason for Diwali

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, celebrates the victory of light over darkness, knowledge over ignorance, and good over evil. It commemorates Lord Rama’s return to Ayodhya after fourteen years of exile and his victory over the demon king Ravana, as told in the epic Ramayana.

The festival symbolizes hope, renewal, and the triumph of righteousness.

Celebrations During Diwali

Diwali is celebrated with lively and colorful traditions including:

  • Lighting diyas (oil lamps) and candles to illuminate homes and public spaces.
  • Fireworks and sparklers to mark the joyous occasion.
  • Rangoli — decorative patterns made at the entrance of homes using colored powders or flowers.
  • Prayer ceremonies dedicated to Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity.
  • Exchanging sweets and gifts among family, friends, and neighbors.
  • Feasting with a variety of traditional delicacies and festive meals.

Overall, Diwali is a joyous occasion that strengthens community bonds and expresses gratitude.
