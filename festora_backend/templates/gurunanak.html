Guru Nanak Gurpurab

Significance of Guru Nanak Gurpurab

Guru Nanak Gurpurab celebrates the birth anniversary of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism and the first of the ten Sikh Gurus. It is one of the most important festivals in the Sikh calendar, symbolizing the birth of spiritual wisdom, compassion, and equality. The festival honors Guru Nanak's teachings that emphasize devotion to God, honest living, and service to humanity.

Celebrations During Guru Nanak Gurpurab

The festival is celebrated with various vibrant traditions including:

  • Akhand Path: Continuous reading of the Guru Granth Sahib for 48 hours.
  • Prabhat Pheris: Early morning processions through the streets singing hymns.
  • Nagar Kirtan: Colorful processions with music, martial arts displays, and community participation.
  • Langar: Community kitchen serving free meals to all visitors.
  • Special prayers and Ardaas: Held in gurdwaras to seek blessings and reflect on Guru Nanak's teachings.

Guru Nanak Gurpurab is a time of spiritual reflection, communal harmony, and celebration of Sikh heritage worldwide.
