Paryushana Mahaparva

Significance of Paryushana Mahaparva

Paryushana Mahaparva is the most important annual Jain festival, observed with great devotion and spiritual discipline. It usually occurs in the month of Bhadrapada (August-September). The festival focuses on self-purification, forgiveness, fasting, meditation, and prayer, culminating in the celebration of Samvatsari, the Day of Forgiveness. Jains ask for forgiveness for any harm caused in thought, word, or deed during the year.

Celebrations During Paryushana Mahaparva

Observances during the festival include:

  • Fasting: Devotees undertake fasts of varying lengths to purify the mind and body.
  • Pratikraman: Ritual of repentance and reflecting on one’s actions.
  • Scripture Reading: Continuous recitation of Jain scriptures such as Kalpa Sūtra.
  • Samvatsari: The final day of the festival when Jains exchange forgiveness with family and community.
  • Temple Visits: Special prayers and discourses in Jain temples.

Paryushana promotes spiritual renewal, ethical living, and community harmony through humility and forgiveness.
