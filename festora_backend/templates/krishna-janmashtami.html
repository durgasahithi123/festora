Krishna Janmashtami

Reason for Krishna Janmashtami

Krishna Janmashtami celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna, one of the most beloved deities in Hinduism. It marks the divine arrival of Krishna to Earth to restore righteousness (dharma) and protect the world from evil. Devotees celebrate with devotion, prayer, and remembrance of his miraculous life and teachings.

Celebrations During Krishna Janmashtami

The festival is celebrated with various traditional customs, such as:

  • Fasting until midnight, the believed time of Krishna’s birth.
  • Bhajans and Kirtans: Devotional singing and dancing in temples and homes.
  • Dahi Handi: A playful event where teams form human pyramids to break a pot of curd hung high, commemorating Krishna’s childhood antics.
  • Decorations: Homes and temples are decorated with flowers, lights, and cradles symbolizing baby Krishna.
  • Recitations: Reading passages from Bhagavad Gita and other scriptures about Krishna’s life.

Krishna Janmashtami inspires devotion, joy, and reflection on divine love and righteousness.
